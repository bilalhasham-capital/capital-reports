Risk by market
All manageable. All already operating.
  • Pakistan — managed risk. SBP 2022 circular named OctaFX, Easy Forex, and Exness — we were not named. Enforcement is payment-channel restriction, not prosecution of traders. SECP has no jurisdiction over offshore operators. We already have 231 FTDs and $213K CBV from 2025 without issue. Brokers and traders have migrated to USDT and e-wallets — which is exactly the first fix.
  • Bangladesh — lowest risk of the three. No offshore CFD broker has ever been named, warned, or fined by BSEC or Bangladesh Bank. No alert list exists. Bangladesh Bank restricts capital outflows via traditional banking — traders use bKash, Nagad, and crypto to route around this. Most permissive environment of the three.
  • Sri Lanka — tolerated, enforcement theoretical. CBSL has a more developed framework (Foreign Exchange Act 2022, EFTC card restrictions) but no offshore broker has ever been actioned. Enforcement targets domestic scam operators, not international regulated brokers. The 6% card acceptance rate is a payments infrastructure problem, not a regulatory one. Sri Lanka is effectively tolerated as long as brokers avoid local physical presence or claiming local regulatory status.
Google Ads is unavailable in all three markets for direct CFD/forex advertising — none are on Google's approved certification list. This is structural, not temporary. Google Search, Display, and any property with direct CFD/leverage product claims are all prohibited. Permitted: brand search, generic financial education content, and app installs via UAC without product claims. Acquisition must be built on IBs, Telegram/social communities, Facebook, TikTok (education-framed), and app store channels.
