Key findings
AI is winning on volume
• 890 FTDs vs 572 human — AI produces 56% more FTDs since inception
• 310 reactivations vs 75 human — AI produces 4.1× more reactivations
• AI FTD trend is accelerating: 37 → 87 → 253 in last 3 months
• ai.camila: 538 FTDs lifetime, best single month 156 (Jan-26)
• ai.daniela: 295 reactivations lifetime, growing to 79/month
Humans are winning on value
• Human CBV/FTD: $337 vs $106 — 3.2× better quality per conversion
• Human CBV/reactivation: $848 vs $116 — 7.3× better quality per event
• achirino generated $192K CBV from 569 FTDs — the programme's highest-value single agent
• grodriguez generated $45.9K CBV from just 38 reactivations ($1,209/react)
• Human retention CBV/react is declining (loss of lioan in Feb-25 was the key driver)
The big question: the March FTD dip
Mar-26 (last complete month) showed AI at 87 FTDs — below Jan's 210 and Nov's 115. But Apr-26 has already rebounded to 253. The Mar dip was likely a list/campaign cycle issue, not structural decline. The AI programme was not "only 87 FTDs ever" — that was a single-month snapshot.
The CBV quality gap explained
The CBV/FTD gap (AI $106 vs human $337) likely reflects client tier differences, not just call quality. AI works through mass-dialled dormant lists; humans (especially achirino) work assigned higher-value books. Tiering the AI contact lists by client CBV potential would be the single highest-impact lever to close this gap.
