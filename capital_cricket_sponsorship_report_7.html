02 — The DRS Opportunity

Why the review moment is different

Most sports sponsorships are logo placements — passive, decorative, easy to ignore. The DRS review is something else entirely. It is a named, owned, recurring editorial moment that stops the game, commands the broadcast, and holds no trading brand sponsorship in any of the three target territories.

What the viewer experiences

A wicket falls. Or an LBW appeal. The fielding captain holds up a T with their hands. The umpire signals upstairs. The game stops.

Every screen in the stadium — and every TV at home — cuts to a branded full-screen graphic. "Decision Pending." The crowd hushes. The broadcast switches to slow-motion replays, Hawk-Eye ball-tracking, UltraEdge waveforms. The third umpire analyses the evidence.

This sequence lasts 30–90 seconds. Every person in the ground and watching on TV is waiting. No other moment in cricket commands more focused, undivided attention.

The commentator says the words: "And they go to the Capital.com Review."

The verdict comes. Red light or green. Out or not out. The Capital.com brand has just been present — calmly, analytically — through the most watched moment of the passage of play. It happens 3–6 times per T20 match. Every match. Every series. Every season.

ICC Cricket World Cup 2019 — DRS review in progress. Sponsors visible below: Nissan, OPPO, MRF Tyres, Booking.com. The "Decision Pending" graphic commands the full stadium screen.

A team pauses. Challenges a decision. Commits a limited resource. Awaits a better outcome based on better information. This is the Decision Review System. It is also, word for word, Capital.com's product promise.

03 — Brand Alignment

Why cricket works with the 2026 brand doctrine

Cricket is not an obvious sports sponsorship for a CFD broker. That is precisely why it works. The sport's DNA — patience, strategy, discipline, the value of not acting — maps onto Capital.com's brand territories with unusual precision. Run through every filter: it passes.

Territory — Pause
"The space between stimulus and action is the product."
A DRS review is a literal pause. The game stops. A decision is being reconsidered with the benefit of better information. A captain chooses to challenge rather than accept the surface read. This is not metaphor — it is the mechanic itself.
Territory — Restraint
"Not acting is a decision. Inaction is a complete outcome."
Reviews are rationed. In T20, each team gets one unsuccessful review per innings. The decision to use — or not use — a review is a strategic act of restraint. Burning a review too early has consequences. So does holding one too long.
Territory — Discipline
"Rules made while calm, enforced under pressure."
The best captains establish review protocols in the dressing room before the pressure of a match. Reactive reviewing — driven by emotion — burns resources. This is the exact parallel to risk management frameworks in trading.
Territory — Clarity
"Not confidence. Not certainty. Reduction of noise."
DRS exists to remove noise from the signal. The on-field umpire's call may be wrong. Technology — Hawk-Eye, UltraEdge — reduces uncertainty. The outcome is not guaranteed certainty; it's better information for a better decision. Identical to Capital.com's product promise.
5 Mandatory Refusal Tests — DRS Sponsorship
✅
Pressure Test
PASS
A DRS review creates deliberation, not urgency. The 15-second window is a moment of considered calm, not compressed action.
✅
Ego Test
PASS
No implication that the viewer is smarter. The review respects the process, not the individual's superiority.
✅
Promise Test
PASS
No outcome is guaranteed. Reviews fail. The system improves decisions — it does not ensure the right one.
✅
Directive Test
PASS
Sponsoring the moment is contextual, not instructional. No behavioural call to action embedded in the format.
✅
Stress Test
PASS
A calm, analytical on-screen moment. Entirely appropriate if a viewer lost money yesterday. No emotional tone.
Brand verdict: DRS sponsorship passes all five mandatory refusal tests — one of the few sports inventory assets that would. The DRS moment is structurally a "pause and reconsider" mechanic. Capital.com sells philosophically the same thing. The alignment is not constructed — it is inherent.

06 — Messaging & Creative

What does Capital.com say in this moment?

All copy and creative direction must pass through the 2026 brand doctrine. The approved vocabulary — pause, restraint, discipline, clarity, judgement — maps directly onto the DRS narrative. Below are production-ready copy lines and creative directions for each territory.

On-screen — Review initiated
"Better decisions compound quietly."
Displays as the review is called. Simple lockup: Capital.com wordmark + approved copyline. Dark background, Warm White text, Gold accent on "Better decisions." No animation beyond a clean fade. Duration: 3–4 seconds.
On-screen — Awaiting verdict
"Between stimulus and action, there is always a gap."
The moment of suspense while Hawk-Eye processes. This line speaks directly to the mechanics — the pause between the appeal (stimulus) and the decision (action). The brand sits in that gap. Precisely the product proposition.
Commentator mention
"And [team] goes to the Capital.com Review."
Verbal branding. The name becomes part of the broadcast vocabulary. This is how it works for "the KFC Six" or "the BKT Tyre Maximum" — the brand is woven into how commentators describe the moment naturally.
Digital / social extension
"Not every decision belongs to today."
Post-match content using DRS review replays. Social posts framed around pivotal review moments — right decision, wrong decision, Umpire's Call. The brand sponsoring not just the moment but the conversation around decision-making under pressure.
OOH / Stadium — Australia
"Discipline is rarely dramatic."
Perimeter board during the review sequence. Australian market — Cluster 1 (Native): individual discipline, autonomous judgement. No urgency, no aspiration. A clean, confident statement. The brand sits in the stadium without competing with the action.
OOH / Stadium — UAE
"Restraint rarely looks heroic."
UAE market — Cluster 2: wealth preservation, long-term horizon. The act of holding a review for the right moment — not wasting it impulsively — is framed as intelligent stewardship. Speaks to the Gulf market's cultural entry point without overt individualism.
07 — How It Actually Works

Integration, process, and activation

How a DRS broadcast sponsorship operates in practice — from commercial negotiation through to on-air execution and digital extension.

Commercial negotiation
Contact the broadcaster's commercial team directly — Fox Cricket or Seven (Australia), Sky Sports (England), Zee (ILT20). The DRS moment is sold as a named broadcast asset on rate card. This is a broadcaster-sold item, not a board-level deal. Faster, lower minimum commitment.
On-screen integration
Capital.com provides a broadcast-spec static or subtle animated bug/overlay in approved brand colours. Triggered by the broadcast director every time a review is called. The commentator names the sponsor: "And they go to the Capital.com Review." Typically 3–6 uses per T20 match.
Social and digital extension
Every review becomes shareable content. Capital.com can license review replay clips for social distribution — "Review of the Match" posts, Hawk-Eye moment breakdowns, decision analysis. Each piece of content carries the Capital.com brand in a naturally analytical frame.
08 — Strategic Recommendation

The recommended path

One move. The most brand-aligned, lowest-friction, highest-differentiation entry point in cricket sponsorship.

The Recommendation

Sponsor the DRS Review moment.

Approach broadcaster commercial teams directly — Fox Cricket / Channel Seven (Australia), Sky Sports (England), Zee / Sony Sports (ILT20 / UAE). The DRS review moment is sold as a named broadcast asset on rate card. This is not a board-level deal. No multi-year commitment required to test.

The ICC is headquartered in Dubai — the same city as Capital.com. Explore ICC global events DRS inventory directly: commercial@icc-cricket.com.

Start with one territory as proof of concept — the 2025–26 Ashes series is the natural entry point given audience scale and brand fit. Scale to ILT20 and ICC global events once established.

Why this, not a shirt deal

Every principal shirt position in the three territories is taken. DRS is the only named broadcast moment in cricket where no trading broker currently holds naming rights across the three target territories.

Estimated entry cost

BBL full season: ~A$300–700K. England international series: ~£400–900K. ILT20 full season: ~$150–350K. All three territories combined for under $2M — vs $10M+ for a principal shirt deal.

What the brand gets

The commentator says "Capital.com Review" 3–6 times per match. The brand is named — not just seen — at the moment of maximum audience attention in every game of cricket it covers.
