Flags & Observations ANOMALIES · STANDOUTS · STRUCTURAL ISSUES
✦ Standout Performance
Qatar Retention — Exceptional CBV/Reactivation
48 reactivations generated $252,921 CBV in 2026 YTD — an average of $5,269 per reactivation. This is 3.5× above UAE Retention's already-strong $1,481/reactivation. Qatar's dormant book contains extremely high-value traders. Retention agent coverage here is underweighted: only 18 effective calls from 3,674 total (0.5% connect rate). The call list quality or timing is the constraint, not the client value.
$5,269 / reactivation · highest in fleet
✦ Programme Leader
UAE Conversion — Dominant Across All Metrics
417 FTDs + $694K FTD CBV makes UAE Conv the single largest revenue engine in the fleet — over 6× the next country (Mexico Conv at $59K). Average FTD deposit value $1,664. Running since Aug 2025, the longest-running market. Marginal connect rate (0.8%) is consistent with high-volume dialling patterns against large lead pools.
$694K CBV · 417 FTDs · $1,664/FTD
🚨 Critical — Review Required
Ghana Conversion — 29K Calls, $94 Total CBV
Ghana Conv is the 4th-largest country by call volume (29,337 calls) but generated only $94 CBV total — effectively zero commercial value. 20 FTDs at $4.70 average deposit suggests either wrong lead pool (micro-retail clients), incorrect agent targeting, or a population with insufficient capital. Retention (203 calls, 0 reactivations) adds nothing. Immediate campaign review recommended — cost-per-call is not justified at current output.
$4.70 avg deposit · CBV/call = $0.003
🚨 Critical — Campaign Efficiency
Nigeria — 11.7K Calls, 1 FTD, $18 Total CBV
Nigeria has been live since Oct 2025 (Conv) yet generated 1 FTD and $18 CBV combined across 11,738 calls. Connect rate of 0.9% is not itself the issue — UAE manages similar rates at scale. The lead book appears to be low-quality or mis-targeted. Retention only launched Apr 2026 so that's embryonic, but Conversion has had 6+ months with no meaningful output. Escalate for lead list audit.
6 months live · $18 total CBV · pause for review
⚠ Structural Mismatch
Morocco Conv — 19% Connect Rate, 2 FTDs
Morocco has the 2nd-highest connect rate in the fleet (19.1% — behind only Oman at 45%) but produced just 2 FTDs from 6,920 conversion calls. High connection rates prove the dialling data is good and clients are picking up. The failure is post-connect: either agent script, offer relevance, or regulatory/payment friction post-call is killing the funnel. Retention ($1,170 CBV from 21 reactivations) is working fine — same clients, different outcome. Conversion script warrants investigation.
19% connect · 0.03% FTD rate · script review needed
⚠ Lead Quality
Saudi Arabia Conv — 33 FTDs, $61/FTD Average
Saudi Arabia conversion has a healthy 6% connect rate and 33 FTDs — but average FTD deposit is $61 vs UAE's $1,664 and Kuwait's $984. This implies either a different lead segment (lower net worth), or the conversion agent is capturing low-intent leads who deposit the minimum. The retention side is healthier ($14.6K from 34 reactivations — $429/reactivation), suggesting the existing book is stronger than the new acquisition funnel.
$61/FTD vs $1,664 UAE benchmark
⚠ Connect Rate Issue
Brazil & Chile Conv — Critical Connect Rate Problem
Brazil Conv: 8,727 calls, 35 effective (0.4% connect rate). Chile Conv: 10,084 calls, 22 effective (0.2% connect rate). Both launched in late 2025 / early 2026. These are among the worst connect rates in the fleet. Likely causes: incorrect phone number format (country code errors), call timing misalignment with local timezone, or the dialler not respecting local DNC/regulatory windows. Chile's retention (6 eff calls from 1,250) has the same problem.
Brazil 0.4% · Chile 0.2% · check dialler config
◆ Structural Anomaly
UK Conversion Agent Generating Reactivation CBV
UK shows 0 FTDs but 7 reactivations and $5,433 CBV attributed to the Conversion team (no Retention agent active). This either means: (A) the conv agent is being directed at dormant traders rather than fresh leads — a misrouting issue; or (B) called clients were existing traders who then became active again within 14 days (counted as reactivations). Worth checking whether UK Conv has a dedicated lead list or is pulling from the broader callable population. No UK Retention agent launched yet.
Conv agent → reactivation outcome · investigate lead source
✦ Operational Excellence
Oman — Fleet's Best Connect Rate on Both Teams
Oman Conversion: 46.0% connect rate (2,608 eff / 5,668 calls). Oman Retention: 42.7% (583 eff / 1,364 calls). These are dramatically above fleet average (~3–5% for most markets). This is not a number-quality artifact — both teams showing the same rate points to excellent lead book hygiene, correct calling hours, and a receptive client base. 48 FTDs + 58 reactivations = $32.8K combined CBV. A template market to study for operational setup.
46% conv connect rate · highest in fleet by far
