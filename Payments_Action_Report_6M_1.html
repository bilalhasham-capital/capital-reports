Possible Solutions
Branded Prepaid Card via BaaS → Own E-Money Licence

A Capital.com-branded prepaid Mastercard would bypass the core PSP problem entirely — clients fund the card directly, money moves within their own account ecosystem rather than through a merchant acquiring flow, eliminating MCC-based issuer blocks. In the near term this can be launched via a Banking as a Service (BaaS) partner: a licensed card issuer (e.g. Gate to Pay, Neopay, Paymentology) provides the regulated infrastructure — card issuing, scheme membership, safeguarding — while Capital.com builds the product and brand layer on top. No additional broker licence required, and the model is already proven in this space by Equiti via Gate to Pay. Weeks to launch, not months. Medium-term, Capital.com's e-money licence in progress would replace the BaaS dependency entirely — bringing card issuance in-house, improving unit economics at scale, and giving full control over the client funding experience. Given $108M in declined deposit volume in UAE alone over the last 6 months, the addressable impact is material.

Local Payment Schemes by Market

Each market has regulated domestic schemes handling the majority of local transactions. These require a PSP with local connectivity to route — several are already accessible via Checkout.com and My Fatoorah. Neteller and Skrill, which are running at near-zero AR across all these markets, should be replaced with these alternatives.

GCC
  • Saudi Arabia — Mada: national debit scheme, dominant across all online transactions. Activatable via My Fatoorah (already our primary KSA PSP). Also STC Pay: telco-backed wallet, 12% of all KSA online payments.
  • Kuwait — KNET: 85%+ of all Kuwait transactions. Checkout.com is already our primary Kuwait PSP and supports KNET — this is a configuration enable only, no new contract needed.
  • Qatar — NAPS (QPay): Qatar Central Bank scheme, 70%+ of Qatar online transactions. Both Checkout.com and Credorax have NAPS connectivity.
  • Oman — OmanNet: ~70% of Oman online transactions. Checkout.com supports OmanNet. Straightforward to enable alongside Credorax.
  • UAE — e& money (Etisalat Wallet): first CBUAE-licensed digital wallet, fast-growing. Complements Lean, which is already live at 77% AR.
Hong Kong
  • FPS (Faster Payment System): HKMA-operated real-time bank transfer system, connects 30+ banks, instant and free. Direct equivalent of Lean for UAE. Checkout.com already supports FPS.
  • AlipayHK + WeChat Pay HK: dominant local wallets, essential for clients with mainland China connections. High adoption among HK's younger demographic.
  • PayMe (HSBC-backed): widely used for retail and P2P payments across the market.
Mexico
  • DiMo: Bank of Mexico's phone-number-based instant transfer layer, 7M+ users in its first year, backed by BBVA and Santander. Accessible via D24, our best-performing PSP in Mexico at 81% AR — worth enabling as a low-friction local option for clients without card access.
  • Mercado Pago: dominant Latin American digital wallet. Worth enabling as a fallback for card-declined clients.
Chile
  • WebPay Plus fix — switch PSP: WebPay is live but at 14% AR ($2.3M declined). This is a PSP routing failure, not a method failure. Credorax and Checkout cannot route WebPay correctly in Chile. dLocal and PayRetailers are Chile specialists — PayRetailers is already running at 100% AR on $433K in our data. Switching WebPay routing to dLocal or PayRetailers is the single highest-impact fix for Chile.
  • Khipu fix — same root cause: live at 26% AR, same misrouting issue. dLocal supports Khipu natively with high acceptance rates. No new method needed — new PSP only.
  • Redcompra: Chile's national debit card scheme (Transbank), not currently enabled. Would give a direct local card rail bypassing international card routing entirely. Supported by dLocal.
  • Fintoc: open banking API connecting directly to Chilean bank accounts — cleaner and more reliable than Khipu. Supported by dLocal. Worth enabling as the long-term local bank transfer solution.
